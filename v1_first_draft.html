Colorectal Cancer - my knowledge map project

This is my project about colorectal cancer incidence and demographics. I am going to put data here.

Basic Facts

  • about 153,000 new cases per year in the US
  • 3rd most common cancer
  • 57,000 deaths per year
  • more common in older people
  • more common in men than women

Who gets it more? (demographics)

I need to look up the exact numbers but basically:

  • Black Americans have higher rates than other groups
  • rates go up a lot after age 50 (now 45)
  • appalachia and south have higher rates
  • young people are getting it more now which is weird

Risk factors I found

Things that increase your risk:

  1. Lynch syndrome (genetic)
  2. FAP (genetic)
  3. IBD / crohns / ulcerative colitis
  4. red meat and processed meat
  5. obesity
  6. alcohol
  7. smoking
  8. family history
  9. type 2 diabetes maybe??
  10. gut bacteria (new research)

Screening

You can screen for it with:

  • colonoscopy (every 10 years)
  • FIT test (every year, stool test)
  • Cologuard (DNA stool test)
  • CT colonography

In 2021 they changed the age to start screening from 50 to 45 because young people are getting it more

Black and Hispanic people get screened less which is bad
